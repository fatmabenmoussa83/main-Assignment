Moana

About The Movie

Moana is a heartwarming animated adventure film released in 2016 by Walt Disney Animation Studios.
Directed by Ron Clements and John Musker, the film is inspired by Polynesian mythology and follows
the journey of a spirited young girl named Moana Waialiki.
Moana, the daughter of the chief on the Pacific island of Motunui, has an adventurous spirit and an
unyielding love for the ocean. Despite her people's long-standing tradition of staying within the
safety of the reef, Moana is drawn to the vastness of the ocean and dreams of exploring beyond her
home's boundaries.
The peaceful life on Motunui is disrupted when a mysterious darkness starts spreading, affecting
the island's resources and livelihood. Moana's grandmother, Tala, reveals to her the forgotten
history of their ancestors, who were voyagers and skilled navigators. They had stopped venturing
beyond the reef due to the loss of their leader, Maui, stealing the heart of Te Fiti, the goddess
of creation. Tala believes that Moana is destined to restore the heart and save her people.
Determined to bring back harmony to her island, Moana embarks on an epic quest to find Maui and
convince him to return Te Fiti's heart. Along her journey, Moana faces various challenges, tests
her own courage and determination, and discovers her true identity as a wayfinder and leader.
During her encounters with Maui, who initially resists helping her, they develop a complex
relationship that evolves as they face various obstacles together. Along the way, Moana learns
important lessons about self-discovery, the importance of embracing one's heritage, and the
significance of finding her own way.
The film's breathtaking animation, powerful music (composed by Lin-Manuel Miranda,
Opetaia Foa'i, and Mark Mancina), and strong storytelling received widespread acclaim.
"Moana" is celebrated for its empowering portrayal of a brave and independent heroine
who defies stereotypes, making it a beloved addition to Disney's animated classics.
The film's themes of courage, self-belief, and connection to one's roots resonate with
audiences of all ages, making "Moana" an enduring and cherished tale.
